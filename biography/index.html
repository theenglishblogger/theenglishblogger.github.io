Biography of Sean McLaughlin

Welcome to my biography page! I am Sean McLaughlin, a passionate web developer and lifelong learner.

When I was younger I spent a lot of my time online. I browsed tons of different websites throughout my childhood. I browsed through blogs, forums, and of course I frequented some social media sites often.

When I was about eight years old I had the idea to write my own blog. So with my 2nd grade writing level, I used to write tons of fictional stories in Microsoft Word. I eventually ditched Microsoft Word and challenged myself to learn HTML, CSS, and JS. After learning a little bit of JavaScript, I spent more of my time learning programming than writing. As YouTube grew in viewers becoming the top website to stream videos online, I eventually found myself watching videos instead of continuing to program.

Thanks to YouTube, I eventually found myself interested in editing video content. I would spend the next six years of my life using Vegas Pro. I made AMVs from anime clips I downloaded from YouTube. I also made Call of Duty Ghosts montage videos that I recorded footage for off the Xbox 360 my parents bought for my sister and I.

I entered high school and started making music using Logic Pro X on my mac, and even ended up learning Ableton, and FL Studio. I was inspired to do this from the videos the YouTube algorithm kept recommending I watched. During high school I took Multimedia courses where my editing skills only got stronger in Premiere Pro. Although I didn't do a lot of work with it, I also learned a bit of Adobe After Effects.

While swimming one day I was thinking about what I wanted to do with my life. I originally planned on apply to colleges undecided. Then I remembered a growing issue almost everywhere worldwide. I remembered cyber attacks.

I remembered times my favorite video games and websites were inaccessible thanks to some bad actors online. I also remembered the hundreds of cyber security videos I watched for a few months that the YouTube algorithm kept recommending me for a while.

I knew that my editing skills could land me a few jobs in advertising, but I thought that I could make a greater impact on the people around me by learning how to help others secure their digital assets. It did not take me much longer to get back on YouTube and practicing my coding again.
